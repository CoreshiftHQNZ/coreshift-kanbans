HOW TO USE VELOCITY

Walking a deal from Prospect to Closed Won

Velocity tracks every deal through six stages — Prospect → Engaged → Qualified → Presented → Verbal → Closed Won — and holds you to gate criteria at every stage that prove you're ready to move forward. This page walks you through the whole motion. Start at the top, work down.

How it works

The three ideas behind every gate

Velocity replaces the patchwork of spreadsheets, sticky notes, and "I'll follow up next week" intentions that used to run your pipeline. No pushing deals on optimism — if the buyer hasn't done the thing that proves real intent, the gate stays closed.

  1. Forcing events. Every stage has one buyer-side action that proves the deal is real — a meeting on the calendar, a verbal yes, a contract signed. Without it, the deal can't progress.
  2. Honest forecasts. A deal is in your forecast only if buyer signals back it up. No phantom pipeline.
  3. Buyer in the loop. Every deal has a portal link the buyer opens to see what you've discussed, confirm what you understood, and review documents you send. Signals flow back automatically.

Your first 10 minutes

Four steps, each stand-alone
Step 1 · 30 seconds

Sign in with Google

Open velocity.coreshifthq.com → click Sign in with Google → pick your Coreshift Google account → grant access. You'll land on the Dashboard. That's your auth done.

Step 2 · 2 minutes

Connect your Google Calendar

This is a separate connector (different OAuth scope than sign-in) — it's what lets Velocity create meetings, send invites, and read buyer RSVPs.

  • Settings (left sidebar, near the bottom)
  • Connections → Google Calendar
  • Click Connect, grant the calendar.events permission

Velocity now creates real events from inside deals, emails invites, tracks RSVPs, and syncs hourly + on-demand via the Sync button on the deal page.

Step 3 · 10 seconds (optional)

Turn on Larger Text

Bumps every label, button, and chip one notch above standard. Settings (left sidebar) → Accessibility → flip Larger Text on. Saves to your account — follows you across devices and survives logging out.

Step 4 · 1 minute

Add your first prospect

Click + New prospect (top-right of the Dashboard or Pipeline page) → pick a playbook → fill in company, contact, value → Create prospect. You'll land on the deal page, ready to work.

The full walkthrough

Every stage has a clear "next move" surfaced at the top of the deal page

The Gate Progress card on every deal shows exactly which criteria still need to be met before you can promote. Auto-checkable criteria turn green on their own as you do the work elsewhere; manual ones you tick yourself.

Stage 1

Prospect → Engaged

What it means: You've identified a target. Nothing happens until you have a first conversation.

Forcing event: A discovery call booked.

What to do: Click Schedule meeting on the deal (Mission CTA up top), pick a date with the buyer's primary contact, send the invite. Once the meeting is on the calendar and the buyer accepts, the gate goes green.

Discovery call already happened? Click Already happened? Log notes — the secondary button next to Schedule meeting. Captures the past date + your meeting notes. Counts toward the gate the same as a scheduled call.

Tip: Capture industry, region, and turnover as you book — the ICP score picks these up automatically.
Stage 2

Engaged → Qualified · 8 criteria

What it means: You've had the first conversation. Now decide whether this deal is real enough to invest more time in.

  • Opening 3 qualifiers (turnover, growth on agenda, challenge area)
  • Lean decision-making — no committee
  • ICP score above threshold
  • Compelling event identified — why now
  • Decision-maker named with access confirmed
  • Pain motivation ≥ 8/10
  • Willingness to invest validated
  • Second meeting (qualification call) on calendar — forcing event

What to do: Open the ICP card and score them. Open Qualifiers and tick what you confirmed on discovery. Mark the decision-maker. Schedule the qualification call.

Tip: Drop a note (button: Add note) summarising the discovery call. Velocity's extractor reads it and pre-ticks several gate criteria for you to confirm. Less form-filling.
Stage 3

Qualified → Presented · 5 criteria (6 on Coreshift HQ)

What it means: They're qualified and ready to see a proposal. The stage is named for the artefact you'll bring.

  • Second meeting happened with the people we said would be there
  • Explicit agreement to receive a proposal
  • Their definition of success captured in their own words
  • Proposal drafted and shared with the buyer ahead of the review meeting
  • Third meeting (proposal review) on calendar — proposal sent ahead — forcing event
  • (Coreshift HQ only) Scope of first-build agreed in principle

What to do: Tick the manual criteria as you confirm them (or drop a note). Draft the proposal — use the Living Proposal section, or attach a PDF and tag it Proposal. Share it with the buyer (eye icon → visible in portal). Schedule the review meeting.

Tip: The buyer can mark the proposal as reviewed in their portal — that signal flows back to you and pre-ticks the next gate's proposal_reviewed. Wait for them to do it; chasing "did you see it?" is the kind of friction Velocity is built to remove.
Stage 4

Presented → Verbal · 3 criteria

What it means: All about buyer commitment. You're not building anything new; you're closing.

  • Proposal reviewed with the decision-maker
  • Verbal yes from the decision-maker
  • Buyer asked for the contract — we didn't have to push — forcing event

What to do: Run the proposal review meeting. Drop a note capturing what was said: "Sam gave a clear verbal yes. Dan asked us to send the contract documents." The extractor pre-ticks two; click to confirm. Manually tick docs_requested — your judgment call.

Tip: The forcing event here is specifically buyer-initiated. If you find yourself sending the contract without them asking, the deal is weaker than it looks. Pause and ask.
Stage 5

Verbal → Closed Won · 3 criteria

  • Buyer signed the contract — forcing event
  • Handover doc passes quality checklist (HARD GATE)
  • Kickoff scheduled

What to do: Send the contract (publish a Living Proposal, or attach + tag Signed contract). When the buyer's signature comes back, click Mark signed under the stage track on the deal. Open the Handover modal and complete all 6 required items: scope locked, stakeholders mapped, kickoff date, exec team assigned, billing setup, success criteria documented. Schedule the kickoff meeting. Click Mark Won on the stage track → fills in win-reason tags → transitions cleanly.

Tip: "Mark Won" is the official close — it creates the win-loss record that drives /win-loss analytics and triggers commission generation. Don't just drag the card; click Mark Won so the reason capture happens. The drag-to-Won path now also routes through the reason-capture modal automatically.

The buyer's view (the portal)

A magic-link URL the buyer opens — no password

Every deal has a buyer portal — a magic-link URL the buyer opens to see:

  • Where the deal is in the process
  • What's been discussed and agreed
  • Documents you've shared
  • Outstanding action items
  • A list of "things to confirm" you've extracted from your conversations

Share the portal link from the deal page (top-right of the Deal hero). The buyer doesn't need a password — the link itself is the access.

Things the buyer can do

  • Confirm or correct facts you've shared
  • Mark shared documents as reviewed (with optional feedback)
  • See the next milestone date
  • Ask a question (routes to you, never bound by the AI on price/scope)

Every buyer action notifies you — bell icon in the left sidebar + per-deal notifications card.

Things worth knowing

Patterns reps hit in QA — surface them before they bite you
  • Rescheduling resets the buyer's RSVP to "needs action" — intentional, they haven't confirmed the new time. Updated invite goes out automatically; chase if the new time was critical.
  • Discovery counts as meeting #1 — the Qualified gate's "second meeting" means meeting #2. If you skip discovery, the count's off — log it as a past meeting to fix.
  • The gate is mostly buyer-driven on purpose. If you find yourself manually ticking a lot of boxes to push a deal forward, slow down. The forcing events are designed to be uncomfortable when the deal isn't real.
  • /win-loss and /commissions only populate from the Mark Won (or Mark Lost) flow. Drag-to-Won now routes through reason capture automatically.
  • "Should we walk?" appears when an ICP disqualifier trips. If a kill is real, click Mark Closed Lost; if you ticked the wrong toggle, click Update ICP score next to it to fix.
