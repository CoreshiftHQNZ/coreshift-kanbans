M1M1-ONBOARD

Business setup + learner invite flow

Parent/child model: CoreShift (or self-service) creates the business account; owner completes a business profile (industry, size, AI platform of choice, tools in use, manager identification, billing contact). Owner invites learners by email; learners set up a login (email/password or magic link in v1) and join the workspace. Owner sees the team populate as learners accept. Optional activator kick-off session is priced separately. Data: Business (owner, managers, tier/billing, shared Cowork projects) → Learner (profile, role, manager, prior AI experience).

M2M2-INTAKE

The intake — conversational interview

The heart of the system. One question at a time, Merlin acknowledging each answer before the next — feels like a chat, never a form. A few minutes. Captures enough about the learner’s real job to design a meaningful pathway, including an explicit “how comfortable are you with AI” read. Must save-and-resume — leave mid-flow and return without losing place. Infers + stores the learner’s confidence/skill level on the profile (drives scaffolding depth later).

M3M3-PATHWAY

Pathway generation + reveal

Merlin reflects back what it heard and generates a personalised module sequence for the learner’s specific role, tools and pain points — then previews it (what’s first and why, what’s coming). This is the “this gets me” delight moment. Architecturally light: pathways are generated fresh per learner, nothing static to maintain. Propose, don’t interrogate — concrete suggestions, not “what do you want AI to do?”

M3M3-TASK

Task screen

Per module: why this matters for their day; what they’ll build; the steps; the ready-to-use prompt with a Copy button; a “here’s what it’ll look like” preview; gentle recovery instructions in every step. Scaffolding depth, prompt verbosity and step granularity flex to the learner’s stored confidence level.

M3M3-HANDOFF

⭐ The hand-off (design this most carefully)

The make-or-break of the product. Narrate the whole round-trip before they move. One-click “Copy this” with an unmistakable confirmation. OS-aware paste tooltip (Cmd+V / Ctrl+V) with a pre-emptive recovery line. Teach side-by-side windows as an early low-stakes win. Don’t make them copy output back — the return is a conversation. Constraint: Merlin has no visibility into the AI tool’s session, so all guidance is given before they leave; the return relies on self-report. Shape depends on the bridge decision.

M3M3-LOOP

The return / loop-closure (“did it work?”)

Warm welcome-back, every time — “Merlin was waiting for me,” never “starting over.” Low-pressure check-in with honest, unpunished options (“Yes, lots / Yes, once / I just tested it / Not yet”), leading into celebration and the next unlock. Loop-closure responses (did they use it, what for, what changed) + a confidence read are the data substrate for levelling + the manager view.

M4M4-PROGRESS

Progression — spells / spellbook (private, non-comparable)

Make everyone feel they’re levelling up, in their own context, privately. No leaderboards, no public scores, no ranking. Ever. Growth framed against the user’s own starting point. Milestones personal + contextual to their role. Levels driven by demonstrated real use (loop-closure + confidence), not time or clicks — and deliberately non-comparable across users at the data level (different roles → different pathways → no shared scale). Spells/spellbook is the playful skin; reconcile with formal accreditation (Ricky’s task).

M4M4-MANAGER

Manager view — outcomes dashboard

A separate, calmer surface. Leads with outcomes, not activity (“five workflows implemented, three hours/week saved” — not logins). Per-person + team roll-up. Privacy boundary is a hard rule, enforced in the data layer: manager sees outcomes, progress and aggregated trends only — never the learner’s actual conversations or individual fumbles. The trust line must be visible in the UI too.

M4M4-WEEKLY

Weekly manager report

The manager centrepiece: a warm, scannable weekly digest in plain language — “a thoughtful update from a good team lead,” not an analytics console. Workflows implemented, time saved, confidence movement, things built and shared. Cadence/trigger + delivery (email/in-app/both) is Ricky’s decision.

M4M4-TRENDS

Frustration / constraint detection

A real capability to build: Merlin watches for recurring signals across learners (the same blocker at loop-closure or in Ask Merlin, a tool/process people keep hitting limits with) and surfaces them to the manager as constructive insight (“several people are blocked by the CRM”) — pattern-spotting over aggregated signals, never one person’s complaint. Candidates for the CoreShift build-idea / nomination pipeline.

M5M5-ASK

Ask Merlin — always-available help

Friendly help outside the task flow, always reachable. Conversations are saved and resumable from the user’s history. A source of frustration/trend signals (M4-TRENDS) when blockers recur here.

M4M4-LEVELDETECT

Pace / level re-evaluation over time

The stored confidence level isn’t static — re-evaluate as the learner demonstrates growing capability, and make it explicitly steerable (“slow down” / “I’ve got this, move faster”) with dignity. Default warm and a touch slow, with an easy way to speed up.
